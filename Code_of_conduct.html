Code of Conduct

For emergencies and personal issues, you must notify Jiarui (Jerry) Cai by sending us a message

For Directors and Managers:

Participation:

  • Directors and managers must abide by the deadline given to them for the corresponding assignments, which includes notes, practice problems, and proofs.
  • Directors and managers must have time management skills in terms of doing the amount of work deemed appropriate per fortnight (two weeks) with the best quality as their progress will be checked every two weeks.
  • Directors and managers must check in on the creators and designers in their correponding department on their progress and the quality of their work monthly; if the creator lacks progress or quality of work, directors and managers must notify the creator about it.
  • Directors and managers must use their original work, which means that they are not permitted to violate others' copyright without others' permission, including from other directors or managers.
  • Directors and managers must declare their contributions to Violintegral as the property of Violintegral in public, their contributions will not be classified as private property.
  • Directors and managers should consult to the founder if they have confusions.

    • Becoming Inactive:

  • Directors and managers who are no longer active will be removed from position with an indication that they are inactive and were once the director and/or manager.

    • Potential Consequences for Violating the Code of Conduct:

  • No directors and managers should violate the Code of Conduct.
  • Directors and managers who violate the Code of Conduct will face consequences based on the Founder's discretion (this includes potential lowering of position to creator or web designer or removal from position with no indication that they were once the director and/or manager).

    • For Creators:

    Participation:

  • Creators must abide by the deadline given to them for the corresponding assignments, which includes notes, practice problems, and proofs.
  • Creators must notify the Subject Directors for a request of up to two months of extension time one month prior to the deadline if they cannot finish by the deadline.
  • Creators who do not start working on their corresponding assignments, which includes notes, practice problems, and proofs, two weeks after being assigned will be removed from position.
  • Creators must have time management skills in terms of doing the amount of work deemed appropriate per month with the best quality as their progress will be checked monthly.
  • Creators must use their original work, which means that they are not permitted to violate others' copyright without others' permission, including from other creators.
  • Creators must declare their contributions to Violintegral as the property of Violintegral in public, their contributions will not be classified as private property.
  • Creators should consult to their Subject Manager if they have confusions.

    • Becoming Inactive:

  • Creators who are no longer active will be indicated as an inactive creator.

    • Potential Consequences for Violating the Code of Conduct:

  • No creators should violate the Code of Conduct.
  • Creators who violate the Code of Conduct will face consequences based on the Subject manager's discretion (this includes potential removal from position with no indication that they were once the creator).

  • Web designers who violate the Code of Conduct will face consequences based on the Website manager's discretion (this includes potential removal from position with no indication that they were once the web designer).
